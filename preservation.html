Food Preservation

Make harvests and groceries go further while keeping flavor and nutrition—choose the method that fits your food and storage space.

Methods & guides

Freezing — lock in flavor & nutrients

Freezing halts microbes and enzymes. Blanch veg, cool, and pack well to avoid freezer burn.

UMN Extension Illinois Extension Frozen Food Foundation Mayo Clinic Health System
Canning — shelf-stable sauces, jams & veg

Water-bath for high-acid; pressure for low-acid. Always follow tested recipes for safety.

Penn State Extension NCHFP (UGA) Ball Mason Jars — Canning Basics Wikipedia
Dehydrating — lightweight snacks

Remove moisture via dehydrator or low oven; store in airtight containers away from light.

NCHFP Backpacking Chef Homesteading Family Healthline
Pickling — tangy + preserved

Quick pickles use vinegar & refrigeration; fermented pickles use salt & time. For shelf storage, can them.

NCHFP Ball: Refrigerator Pickles Old Farmer’s Almanac Wikipedia
Preserving in oil — herbs & veg (safety!)

Infused oils are delicious but perishable. Refrigerate and use within 3–4 days or freeze; dried ingredients store safer.

Penn State Extension OSU Extension The Kitchn — Freeze Herbs in Oil Free Your Fork (“Herb Bombs”)
