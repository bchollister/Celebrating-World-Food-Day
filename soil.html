Soil Amendments & Conditioners

Healthy soil = better harvests. Build structure and biology with compost and targeted amendments, guided by a soil test.

Getting oriented

Three Sisters (Corn, Beans, Squash): a companion-planting trio where corn acts as a living trellis, beans fix nitrogen for the group, and squash shades the soil to suppress weeds and conserve moisture. Read more at the USDA National Agricultural Library: Three Sisters.

Household “kitchen hack” amendments

Everyday Household Garden Amendments & Soil Conditioners
ItemHow to PrepareBenefitsBest For
Used coffee groundsDry or mix fresh into soil/compostAdds nitrogen, organic matter, mild acidityAcid-loving plants (azaleas, gardenias, blueberries)
Tea leaves / bagsRemove staples, compost or mix into soilOrganic matter, mild acidityAcid-loving plants
Pine needlesUse fresh or dry as mulchLong-lasting mulch, retains moisture, acidifies soilAcid-loving shrubs, berries
Citrus peelsChop/blend, bury shallowlyMild acidity, scent deters pestsAcid-loving plants, pest control beds
Crushed eggshellsRinse, dry, crush to powderAdds calcium, slowly raises pHTomatoes, peppers, alkaline-lovers
Baking sodaSprinkle lightly on soil surfaceQuick pH boost in small areasAlkaline-loving plants, mildew prevention

Why these? They’re common, cheap materials that add organic matter or nudge pH—handy for containers and small beds.
