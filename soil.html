Getting oriented

Three Sisters (Corn, Beans, Squash): corn acts as a living trellis, beans fix nitrogen, and squash shades the soil. Learn more: USDA NAL — Three Sisters.

The Three Sisters — A Native Planting Tradition

An ancient, tried-and-true method for growing three staple crops in harmony, improving soil health, and ensuring a sustainable harvest.

How it works: Corn grows tall; beans climb and fix nitrogen; squash covers the ground, conserving moisture and suppressing weeds.

Planting tip: Hill 4–5 corn seeds; when 6–8" tall, add 2–3 bean seeds around each clump and 1–2 squash seeds at the edge.

Best for: family plots, low-input beds, heat-prone gardens

Chaos Gardening — Let Nature Take the Lead

Broadcast diverse seeds and let the strongest thrive. This boosts biodiversity, resilience, and surprise harvests.

How to try it: Mix radish, arugula, calendula, nasturtium, beans, lettuce; scatter in a prepared bed; thin loosely for paths.

Best for: small spaces, low maintenance, pollinator-friendly planting
